The History of Computers

Imagine a world without cell phones,computers,internet, etc. Now imagine building a world with all of it. Both are unimaginable, but yet it happened. It took centuries to get to where the world is today, regarding modern technology, and yet technology is still getting more and more advanced and powerful. How did it all start? Who came up with this idea and made the things that we can't live without happen? There are thousands of answers and thousands of people who created the pathway for our modern day creations.

The first appearance of any form of “technology” was in China and was called an abacus. The abacus, originally made out of beans or stones that moved in grooves on sand or wood frames, is a counting device that can only do basic subtraction and addition and nothing more. It was used by merchants, traders, and clerks and can still be seen in use today in Russia,China, Africa, and Eastern Europe. This was soon though traded out for a better device named the Hindu-Arabic numeral system.

Centries later a new progression was made towards the advancement of math devices. John Napier, a mathematician known for his invention of logarithms and Napier bones. Napier bones was a step up from the abacus because it focused on multiplication, a more complicated subject in math, instead of addition and subtraction. This created a multiplication table to help solve problems easily.

Moving on from the more basic side of math, in the 1800s the newest craze were punch cards, this focused on vital statistics tabulation. Herman Hollerith, the original inventor, was influenced by Jacquard and his use for controlling Jacquard Looms. Jacquard came up with the idea to use holes punched in cardstock to control the pattern looms weave. This was also very influential to Charles Babbage who came up with his own ideas regarding punch cards. Charles Babbage used punch cards to control the sequence of computations in his proposed analytical machine. This was crucial towards Herman Hollerith's idea and of claiming patent rights n storing data on punch cards. Overall, this was a big advancement and it stuck for years leading up to today.

In conclusion, previous actions in history have proved to influence and have a hand in modern day technology. The first computer, the ENIAC, was created as a secret government project at the University of Pennsylvania. The ENIAC was introduced in 1946 and could solve mathematical problems with high speed, while taking up more than 1800 square feet, this “mathematical brain” became the bases of how our computers were invented today. Steve Jobs, the creator of Apple, started out in a garage and created an empire. Without previous discoveries and creations that wouldn't have been able to happen. The history of computers has helped create the advancement in technology. It is important to know how it all started.
